Travel. Eat. Family.

Eating my way through Hong Kong and Taiwan.

Tong Sui

A variety of tong sui, a Hong Kong seewt soup dessert. Made with Chinese brown sugar stick for the soup base; you can add taro, yams, sweet potatoes, red beans, and many more.

Shrimp Jambalaya

This was in Taiwan at a shopping center. It was the first time I had ever eaten jambalaya.

Taiwanese Braised Beef Noodle Soup

One of Taiwan's most famous dish. The beef is so tender tht it literally melts in your mouth. It was the first dish I ate when I arrived in Taiwan.

Dandan Noodles

Jam-packed full of flavors. The noodles were very chewy!

Taiwan City View at Night

This photo was taken at the top floor of Taipei 101. Beautiful view. However, I started getting nauseous as we got higher and higher.

Family Photo in Hong Kong

I traveled with my mom and sisters for this trip. This photo was taken in Hong Kong, at a roof top/balcony area of a mall.

High Tea at The Langham Hotel, Hong Kong

My first High Tea experience and it was at the Langham Hotel. The hotel was super luxurious and huge! We were only there to eat!

Rose Jam

This was served during my high tea experience. I was introduced to the wonderful flavor of Rose Jam. I ve since tried to find it in America, but so far have not been able to.

Family Reunion

I was reunited with my mom side of the family in Hong Kong. They lived in China and we had only seen them once a fews back, so we encouraged them to come to Hong Kong to spend some time together.

Toasteria Cafe, Taiwan

We found this cute little cafe in Taiwan that served pasta and sandwhiches. It was the perfect place to get a break from Asian Cuisine. It was here that we also disovered Rose Latte.

Mango Shaved Ice

The mangos were so sweet and juicy. A perfect dessert for a hot and humid summer day in Taiwan!

Tonkastu Ramen

Delicous tonkastu ramen! We had to wait in line for almost an hour.

Hong Kong Style Egg Tarts

All I remember was having to walk up these tiny steep stairs for about an hour just to get to this famous little bakery. It was so hot that day! We were all hungry, sweaty, and sore!

Matcha Swirl

Matcha Heaven! We were near the port at one of the malls. We got some ice cream and went back out to enjoy the night view of Hong Kong.

Coffee at the Lounge

We were tired from exploring Hong Kong, but didn't want to head back to our rooms. So we stayed in our hotel lounge and tried their menu.

Wonton Soup

f I remember correctly, this was actually the first dish we ate in Hong Kong. We had just arrived and checked into our hotel. This restaurant was about 2 minutes away from our hotel.

Brown Sugar Boba

Of course, we could not pass on the opportunity to try Boba Milk Tea in its home place. It was the best I ever had! I'm salivating just thinking about it. I can't wait to go back for more!

Black Sesame Tong Sui

Very sweet black sesame dessert soup in Hong Kong. It can look a bit intimidating, but I promise it tastes amazing! Go ahead and give it try!

Tonkastu Ramen

Rice noodles with seafood meatballs. Nice chewy hand pulled noodles!
